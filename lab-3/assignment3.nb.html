Statistical Inference with Exponential Distributions

Vlad, Dmytro, Dmytro

February 11, 2024

Overview

This report aims to investigate the properties of the exponential distribution and its relation to the Central Limit Theorem (CLT). We focus on the distribution of the means of 40 exponential distributions, with a rate parameter () of 0.2, across 1000 simulations. The report explores the alignment of the sample means and variances with their theoretical counterparts and illustrates how the sample mean distribution conforms to a normal distribution, as predicted by the CLT.

Simulations

The following R code is used to generate 1000 samples of 40 exponential distributions, each with a rate parameter of 0.2.

lambda <- 0.2
simulations <- replicate(1000, rexp(40, lambda))
sample_means <- apply(simulations, 2, mean)

Sample Mean versus Theoretical Mean

We compare the sample mean of the simulated distributions to the theoretical mean of an exponential distribution.

Theoretical Mean

The theoretical mean of an exponential distribution is 1/.

theoretical_mean <- 1 / lambda
theoretical_mean
[1] 5

Sample Mean

The sample mean is computed from our simulations.

mean_of_sample_means <- mean(sample_means)
mean_of_sample_means
[1] 5.030827

Histogram of Sample Means

The histogram below shows the distribution of sample means.

hist(sample_means, main="Histogram of Sample Means", xlab="Sample Mean")

Sample Variance versus Theoretical Variance

The variance of the sample means is compared with the theoretical variance of the distribution.

Theoretical Variance

The theoretical variance for the mean of 40 exponential distributions is (1/)^2 / 40.

theoretical_variance <- (1 / lambda)^2 / 40
theoretical_variance
[1] 0.625

Sample Variance

The variance of the sample means is computed.

sample_variance <- var(sample_means)
sample_variance
[1] 0.6507858

Distribution

We examine whether the distribution of sample means is approximately normal.

Histogram with Density

The histogram is overlaid with a density plot.

hist(sample_means, main="Histogram of Sample Means with Density", xlab="Sample Mean", freq=FALSE)
lines(density(sample_means), col="blue")

Q-Q Plot

A Q-Q plot is used to visually assess the normality.

qqnorm(sample_means)
qqline(sample_means, col="red")

Conclusion

The histogram and Q-Q plot suggest that the distribution of sample means is approximately normal, in accordance with the Central Limit Theorem. This demonstrates that even for non-normal distributions like the exponential distribution, the mean of a large number of samples will tend to follow a normal distribution.
